Balance

The first way this panel conveys the principle of balance is because the background is lighter than the circles.

The second way this panel conveys the principle of balance is because the five circles are the same size and are equally spaced on the same arc within the panel.

Proportion

This panel conveys the principle of proportion because of the significant (2X) size difference between the two circles.

Rhythm

The first way this panel conveys the principle of rhythm is through progressive rhythm because each adjacent circle is smaller or larger than the other.

The second way this panel conveys the principle of rhythm is through alternating rhythm because the four circles alternate between high and low contrast inner and outer circles.

Emphasis

The first way this panel conveys the principle of emphasis is through isolation because the emphasized circle is separated from the other three circles.

The second way this panel conveys the principle of emphasis is through contrast because the emphasized circle is darker in color than the other three circles.

Unity Through Repetition of Color

The first way the composition is unified is through the repetition of color.

This is because each individual panel exhibits a monochromatic color scheme while the composition, as a whole, exhibits a tetradic color scheme.

Unity Through Repetition of Shape

The second way the composition is unified is through the repetition of shape.

The first way the composition is unified through the repetition of shape is because the shapes within each individual panel are circular.

The second way the composition is unified through the repetition of shape is because the shape of the composition, as a whole, is circular.

Unity Through Continuation

The third way the composition is unified is through continuation.

This is because all of the circles connecting the individual panels are the same size, which helps lead the eyes from one panel to the next.
