Design Principles: Composition

Scale and Proportion—the main design principle that I used in this composition was scale and proportion. First, I reduced the scale from 2 inches to 1 centimeter for the orthographic views and the trace layer. Then, once I completed production on the color, patina, and shadow layers, I scaled the perspective portion to 200%.

Balance—the smaller orthographic views were balanced vs the larger perspective view by using the arrows and by using the gestalt principle of proximity.

Gestalt—the gestalt principles that I used were proximity and common region. The orthographic views were placed near each other (proximity) while the composition was placed within the black border (common region).

Design Principles: The Object

Unity—repetition of shape (rectangles) and color (brown and black).

Scale and Proportion—the object itself is a scaled down representation of a bird house.

Emphasis and Focal Point—the doorway is placed at the center of the front face.

Balance—the doorway, windows, and stars are placed symmetrically on the front face.

Rhythm and Motion—the patina gives the piece a sense of motion as it provides the eyes a break from the hard edges produced by the many rectangles.
